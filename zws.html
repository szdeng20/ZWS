Food Waste Segregation Project – Key Insights

Student analysis of F&B tenants in Clementi Mall shows how operational practices influence Scope 3 emissions, especially:

  • Scope 3 Category 1 – Food purchased but wasted
  • Scope 3 Category 4 – Waste transport and incineration

Key Analytical Insights

  • 🍽 58% of waste comes from customer leftovers, not kitchen preparation.
  • 🏪 Smaller restaurants face major space constraints that prevent adding segregation bins.
  • ⚡ Operational speed during peak hours discourages segregation.
  • 📢 Awareness exists but implementation drops over time.

Scope 3 Emissions Impact

If 11 restaurants segregate food waste into composting instead of incineration:

  • 34.5 tonnes food waste annually
  • ≈ 19 tonnes CO₂ avoided
  • Equivalent to emissions from ~4 cars per year

Better segregation directly reduces Scope 3 waste emissions and transport-related emissions.

Top Recommended Solutions

  1. 🤖 Monthly chatbot feedback showing waste recycling performance
  2. 🗑 Small general-waste bins on customer tables
  3. 🏷 Visual stickers prompting correct disposal behaviour
  4. ♻ Two smaller kitchen bins replacing one mixed bin

Main Strategic Takeaway

Food waste segregation failure is not primarily a behaviour problem. It is a system design problem.

  • Kitchen workflow must integrate segregation naturally
  • Customers must be part of the solution
  • Continuous feedback improves compliance

When segregation becomes easy and visible, Scope 3 emissions drop without increasing operational burden.
